# Timeline

This document summarizes the long-term evolution of the project ecosystem.

The underlying question has remained remarkably stable for more than thirty years:

> How can autonomous agents coordinate without creating a capturable centre?

Over time, this evolved into a broader civilizational question:

> How can humanity preserve its capacity to explore possible futures in the age of industrialized cognition?

---

# 1995 — C.O.R.S.I.C.A.

Creation in Corte of:

**C.O.R.S.I.C.A.**
(*Corse Organisant la Réunion Sur Internet de Compétences Autonomes*)

The objective was already:
- distributed coordination,
- autonomous competencies,
- internet-mediated cooperation,
- and anti-centralized organization.

This predates:
- blogs,
- wikis,
- social media,
- and the formalization of open source culture.

---

# 1998–2001 — Odisei / VoIP / Distributed Discovery

Participation in Odisei:
- employee #1,
- CTO,
- later CEO of the French subsidiary after acquisition by 8x8 Inc.

Main contribution:
- autodiscovery infrastructure,
- decentralized endpoint coordination,
- reduction of centralized dependency.

Patent:
- US7606221B2

The architectural problem remained the same:
coordination without centralized capture.

---

# 2000s — Distributed Systems and Political Reflection

Progressive convergence between:
- technical infrastructure,
- governance problems,
- democratic questions,
- and institutional fragility.

Growing focus on:
- transparency,
- traceability,
- democratic reversibility,
- and distributed coordination.

---

# 2021–Present — Inox

Development of:
- Inox,
- a concatenative virtual machine for distributed systems.

Main themes:
- strict control/data separation,
- reactive systems,
- distributed execution,
- edge computing,
- anti-capture architecture.

---

# 2022–Present — AI Coordination Systems

Development of several AI-related projects:
- Serra,
- Gabriel,
- Ubikial,
- Mailai.

Emergence of:
- sovereign digital twins,
- personal cognitive augmentation,
- and democratic AI governance questions.

---

# 2024 — Political Acceleration

Legislative candidacy in Haute-Corse with Marie-Louise Robert.

Growing realization that:
- infrastructure,
- governance,
- cognition,
- and democracy
could no longer be treated separately.

---

# 2024–Present — After Marie-Louise

The death of Marie-Louise Robert profoundly transformed the project ecosystem.

The questions of:
- transmission,
- memory,
- institutional continuity,
- and civilizational resilience
became central.

Acceleration of:
- Fondation Barons Mariani,
- digital twin research,
- transmissible memory systems,
- and cognitive continuity work.

---

# 2025–Present — MareNostrum / DHITL / FractaVolta

Convergence of:
- energy sovereignty,
- AI governance,
- distributed compute,
- democratic infrastructure,
- and cognitive sovereignty.

Main ideas:
- photon-to-inference infrastructure,
- democratic compute governance,
- anti-capture architecture,
- civilizational optionality,
- one human, one voice.

---

# 2026 — Possibilism

Explicit formalization of:
- Possibilism,
- cognitive sovereignty,
- and democratic AI infrastructure.

The ecosystem increasingly converges toward:

- distributed infrastructure,
- open systems,
- democratic reversibility,
- cognitive symmetrization,
- and preservation of humanity’s exploratory capacity.

---

# Ongoing Direction

The work remains intentionally:
- experimental,
- open,
- iterative,
- and infrastructural.

The objective is not prediction.

The objective is preserving humanity’s capacity to choose.
